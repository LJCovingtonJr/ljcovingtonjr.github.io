About

This page contains some information about me and my personal life, including my less academic pursuits and interests.

My Name

Keeping with the practice—popular among those working with language—of describing how my name is pronounced, I’ve decided to do so here. My full name, LaRoyce Jermaine Covington, Jr., is pronounced:

/ lɑ: ˌrɔɪs dʒərˈmeɪn ˈkəvɪŋtən / ˈdʒuːnjə /

Please forgive my poor attempt at IPA.

My first name, LaRoyce, comes from proud African-American naming traditions.

I often prefer to go by my middle name, Jermaine, from the French Germain which in turn comes from the Latin Germanus, meaning “brother”.

My surname, Covington, has a disputed early history, with Scottish, English, and Latin having some claim to the name.

Finally, I keep a suffix—Junior. This simply indicates that I share my name with my father, who keeps the corresponding suffix Senior.

Model United Nations

Yes, I was one of those people in high school who really liked yelling at people in structured debate. I was one of the dreaded Model United Nations delegates. I participated in the club in high school for all four years and then volunteered with the Model UN organization in my native Volusia County, Florida.

More recently, I’ve served on the staff of the Florida High Schools Model United Nations and their associated conferences.

Judo

In the Fall 2019 semester, I started practicing Judo as a member of the USF Judo Club. I’ve had a fantastic time with the club so far and it’s gone a long way towards getting me into shape (though COVID-19 is not helping).

Political Activities

Did you expect a political science major to not engage in politics? At USF, I am an active member of the Young Democratic Socialists of America and served as the chapter’s President for the 2018-19 academic year. Since then, I’ve served on the chapter’s Steering Committee, the administrative and managing arm of the club. I’ve also served as the club Secretary and as Chair of the Electoral Working Group, which co-ordinates the club’s political activites in the Tampa Bay Area.

In addition to my work with YDSA, I have been active with my university’s chapter of the College Democrats of America. I served as a delegate to the 2017 Florida Democratic Party Convention.
